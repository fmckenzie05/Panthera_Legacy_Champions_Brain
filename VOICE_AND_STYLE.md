# VOICE_AND_STYLE

How Intellusia writes and talks. Use this when generating content, copy, demos, or proposals so the output sounds like us.

Related:
- [[OPERATOR_PROFILE]]
- [[MEMORY]] (messaging memory section)

## Voice attributes

- **Sharp.** Short sentences over long. One claim per sentence.
- **Specific.** Named components ([[Nebuchadnezzar]], [[The Construct]], [[Zion]]), named technologies (Morpheus, Azure, AKS), named outcomes (lateral movement detection, tenant isolation).
- **Technically serious.** We sound like operators, not marketers. No "revolutionary", "next-gen", "world-class".
- **Commercially aware.** Every public sentence either earns trust, frames a buyer outcome, or moves a sale.
- **Confident, not boastful.** Claims are precise enough that a skeptic could verify them.

## Defaults

- Active voice over passive.
- Concrete nouns over abstractions.
- Architecture-backed claims over adjectives.
- Buyer-facing outcome before vendor-facing capability.

## Banned phrases

- "AI-powered" (every vendor says this; we name the technique).
- "Next-generation" / "cutting-edge" / "revolutionary".
- "Leverages" (use "uses").
- "Solutions" as a noun.
- Anything implying autonomous defense if the design is detection/advisory.

## Preferred phrases

- "Multi-tenant AI-security platform built on Azure and NVIDIA Morpheus."
- "Detects [specific behavior] in [specific environment]."
- "Honeynet-derived intelligence" instead of "decoy data".
- "Predictive prioritization" instead of "smart alerts".

## Long-form structure

For posts, pages, and proposals:

1. One-sentence claim that names a buyer outcome.
2. One paragraph describing the architectural reason it works (Morpheus, multi-tenant, Oracle, etc.).
3. One concrete example or component reference.
4. One call to action.

Anything longer earns its length by adding architecture detail or a real example, not adjectives.

## Short-form structure

For social posts and one-liners:

- Lead with a specific observation about the AI-security or AI-build market.
- Tie it to one named [[Anomalie Grid]] component or one [[WORLDVIEW]] belief.
- End with a sharp implication, not a CTA.

## Tone calibration

- **Founder voice (long-form):** thoughtful, opinionated, technical-but-readable.
- **Sales voice (proposals, pages):** outcomes first, architecture as proof.
- **Operator voice (internal docs, this vault):** terse, structural, link-heavy.

When in doubt, write the way [[MEMORY]] is written: structured, specific, no fluff.
