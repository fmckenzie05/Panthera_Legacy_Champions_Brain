# OPERATOR_PROFILE

This is the operator stand-in for Intellusia Studios. The point of this note is **self-duplication**: anyone (human teammate, contractor, or AI agent like Kratos) reading this should be able to make decisions that look and feel like the founder's, without having to ask.

If you are reading this for the first time, also read:
- [[MEMORY]] for company context
- [[DECISION_PRINCIPLES]] for how decisions get made
- [[WORLDVIEW]] for what we believe about the market
- [[OPERATING_RHYTHM]] for how work flows
- [[VOICE_AND_STYLE]] for how we write and talk

## Who the operator is

- **Role:** Founder/operator of Intellusia Studios.
- **Technical center of gravity:** AI + cybersecurity, with strong cloud (Azure-heavy), automation, and data-pipeline experience.
- **Commercial orientation:** Treats engineering as a revenue lever, not an art project. Wants opinions, sequencing, and outcomes — not infinite optionality.
- **Working style:** Architecture-first thinker, but allergic to ideas that never ship. Prefers compressed, sharp output over long deliberation.

## What the operator optimizes for

In rough priority order:

1. **Revenue path** — anything that moves Intellusia closer to a paying client.
2. **Flagship clarity** — sharpening [[Anomalie Grid]] into something a buyer can grasp in 60 seconds.
3. **Execution velocity** — converting designs and PRDs into shippable slices.
4. **Audience and credibility** — content, demos, and architecture writing that earns trust.
5. **Operational leverage** — automation via [[ScriptLab_I3/Studio_Workflow]] so the operator's time is not the bottleneck.

## How to act on the operator's behalf

When making a decision in this vault or on behalf of Intellusia, ask in this order:

1. Does it move one of the five priorities above? If not, deprioritize.
2. Is it consistent with [[DECISION_PRINCIPLES]]?
3. Does it preserve the messaging guardrails in [[MEMORY]] (no overclaiming production readiness, keep Matrix-themed component names, stay specific)?
4. Would the operator recognize the output as "in their voice" per [[VOICE_AND_STYLE]]?

If yes to all four, proceed. If unsure, write the draft and flag the uncertainty clearly.

## What the operator delegates

- Routine connector work and integrations -> [[ScriptLab_I3/Studio_Workflow]] (agentic delivery stack)
- Market signal scanning -> [[WORLDVIEW]] hub (Part 2 — outward radar)
- Repeatable research and lead intel -> [[Government Contracting Pipeline]]
- Content drafting, positioning, sales-asset writing -> Kratos via [[MEMORY]]

## What the operator does NOT delegate

- Final positioning of [[Anomalie Grid]]
- Pricing, contract terms, and direct client conversations
- Architectural decisions that change the product story
- Brand voice on public-facing channels

A good operator stand-in should be able to answer:

- What is Intellusia selling, and to whom? See [[MEMORY]] and [[Buyer Personas]].

- How do clients actually touch the product? See [[CLIENT_CONNECTION]].
- What trends should be steering the build? See [[WORLDVIEW]] (Part 2 — outward radar).

If any of those answers feel thin, update the linked note rather than answering from memory.
