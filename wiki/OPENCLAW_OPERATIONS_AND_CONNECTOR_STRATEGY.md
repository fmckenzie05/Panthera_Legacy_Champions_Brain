# OpenClaw Operations and Connector Strategy

## Summary

OpenClaw should be treated as the operations and connector layer in the Intellusia system, not just a technical side tool.

## Why it matters

If Anomalie Grid is the flagship product story, [[OpenClaw]] is the practical operations surface that connects tools, channels, providers, and infrastructure into a usable working system.

## Details

### Current role

[[OpenClaw]] is best understood as the operational glue layer for external applications and service connectors.
In the vault, it complements the product-centered graph by representing how execution and automation actually touch the outside world.

### Why this matters strategically

This node helps Intellusia avoid becoming only an ideas-and-architecture company.
It creates a place in the system map for:

- messaging channels
- AI providers
- MCP and tooling integrations
- hosting and deployment surfaces

### Practical interpretation

OpenClaw should stay distinct from [[Anomalie Grid]]:

- [[Anomalie Grid]] = flagship product and market-facing architecture
- [[OpenClaw]] = operations, integrations, and execution surfaces

That separation makes the vault easier to reason about and keeps strategy cleaner.

### Immediate priorities

1. document the most important external connectors clearly
2. keep provider, application, and infrastructure nodes distinct
3. use OpenClaw notes to capture real workflows, not just static topology
