# GOV_TECH_TRENDS

Federal/state procurement and AI policy trends that affect Intellusia's gov-contracting capability and any future federal pursuit of [[Anomalie Grid]].

Parent: [[WORLD_VIEW]]
See also: [[Government Contracting Pipeline]], [[GOVERNMENT_CONTRACTING_PIPELINE_STRATEGY]]

## Trend list

### AI use-case inventories and federal AI guidance

- **What:** Agencies publishing AI use-case inventories and procurement guidance.
- **Why it matters:** Signal source for opportunity targeting via the ETL pipeline.
- **Status:** watching as a signal source.

### Zero-trust mandates

- **What:** Federal mandates pushing zero-trust architectures.
- **Why it matters:** [[Anomalie Grid]] story aligns naturally; honeynet + identity + telemetry is a zero-trust narrative.
- **Status:** affects pitch for federal buyers.

### CMMC and supply-chain security requirements

- **What:** Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification expanding into the DIB.
- **Why it matters:** Long-term moat for Intellusia if pursued; expensive to enter.
- **Status:** watching, capacity-gated.

### Small-business set-asides and SBIR/STTR

- **What:** Set-aside vehicles for small AI/cyber firms.
- **Why it matters:** Direct path to first federal revenue without prime contractor dependency.
- **Status:** active interest.

### Agency-level data marketplaces

- **What:** Federal data sources (SAM.gov, USAspending, FPDS) becoming richer and more API-friendly.
- **Why it matters:** Direct input for the Government Contracting ETL pipeline. Capability play.
- **Status:** core to gov pipeline.
